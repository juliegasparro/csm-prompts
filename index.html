Welcome! This library is a collection of ready-to-use AI prompt templates designed specifically for Customer Success Managers. Each prompt helps you create professional, consistent communication in less time — without starting from a blank page. Whether you are writing a follow-up email after a customer call, preparing for a QBR, or handing off an account, these templates give you a reliable starting point you can customize to fit your voice and your customer.

Template 1
Follow-Up Email
Turns rough call notes into a polished customer email with summary, action items, and next steps.
Role: Act as an experienced Enterprise Customer Success Manager. Task: Write a professional follow-up email to my customer based on the call notes below. Include: 1. A brief, warm opening that references the meeting 2. A 3-bullet summary of key discussion points 3. A clear action items list (owner + due date for each) 4. A friendly closing with next meeting date if known Tone: [e.g. warm and direct / formal and concise / collaborative] Relationship context: [e.g. new account / long-term partner / at-risk] Call notes: [PASTE YOUR ROUGH NOTES HERE]

How to use

  1. Copy the prompt above
  2. Replace the [bracketed fields] with your specifics
  3. Paste your raw call notes at the bottom
  4. Paste the whole thing into Claude and send

Style Block

Wrap-Up & Next Steps [Add your style block here]

Template 5 · Step 1 of 3
External Research
Generates source-backed competitive and industry insights to anchor your value framework. Run this first.
I am a Customer Success Manager at [Your Company]. My customer is [Company Name / Industry — anonymize if using public AI]. Research the following and return source-backed insights: 1. What are the top 3–5 business outcomes their customers care most about? 2. What are the most common pain points that drive their customers to buy? 3. What metrics do companies in this industry use to measure success? 4. What competitive pressures or market trends are most relevant? Format: Bullet points with a brief source reference for each insight. Focus on publicly available data. Do not fabricate statistics.

How to use

  1. Fill in your company and your customer's company/industry
  2. Run this in Claude — save the output
  3. Feed that output into the O2 Framework prompt (Template 6) as the next step
Template 6 · Step 2 of 3
O2 Framework Mapping
Maps your research into a 6-column Value Realization Framework table using the O2 methodology.
Role: Act as a strategic Customer Success advisor. Task: Using the research insights below, build a Value Realization Framework using the O2 methodology. Map each insight to these six columns: 1. Value Driver — the overarching business goal 2. Outcome — the specific change the customer wants to achieve 3. Solution — the feature or capability that addresses it 4. Impact — the broader business effect 5. Metric — how success is measured (use quantified examples) 6. Source — where the insight or data came from Return the framework as a table with one row per value driver. Aim for 4–6 rows. Use concrete, business-language descriptions. Do not fabricate metrics — use ranges or "to be measured" if unknown. Research insights: [PASTE YOUR STEP 1 OUTPUT HERE]

How to use

  1. Run Template 5 (External Research) first and save the output
  2. Paste that output into the field at the bottom of this prompt
  3. Claude returns a formatted table — use it in your QBR or account plan
  4. Then use Template 7 to share the framework with your customer
Template 7 · Step 3 of 3
Share the Framework
Writes the customer-facing email introducing your Value Realization Framework and proposing a review call.
Role: Act as an experienced Enterprise Customer Success Manager. Task: Write a short email to my customer introducing the value framework I have built for their account. The email should: - Briefly explain what a Value Realization Framework is and why it matters - Invite them to review the attached framework and share their input - Propose a 30-minute call to walk through it together - Set a collaborative, strategic tone — this is a partnership document Context: - Customer: [Company Name] - Relationship stage: [e.g. 6 months in, renewal in Q3] - Primary contact: [Title, not name] [Add your style block here]

How to use

  1. Fill in the customer and relationship context
  2. Append your Style Block for consistent voice
  3. Claude drafts the email — attach your framework and send
Full workflow: External Research (T5) → O2 Framework Mapping (T6) → Share the Framework (T7). These three prompts are designed to chain together.
